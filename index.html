The average person can read at 150 words per minute. Our brains can process information much faster than our eyes can scan. Conventional reading requires coordination between multiple mental systems. You have to first see the word, retrieve its meaning and its context, relate the word to the rest of the sentence, and then figure out where to point your eyes next. Speed reading trainers train people to read whole passages by scanning text in a zigzag pattern, for example. These methods suggest that our eyes make wasted movements when flitting back and forth over words; by streamlining where we look, we can thereby accelerate our reading. Speed Read, an android app, trains your brain to double your reading and retention speed, overnight.
